Trade War Briefing — April 2026
Where we stand on tariffs

The US-China trade war has entered a new, more institutionalized phase. Peak tariff rates of 145% have been walked back through bilateral negotiations, with Chinese goods now sitting at approximately 47% effective tariff. A separate 10% fentanyl-related tariff was cut as part of a broader deal that also included Beijing pausing rare earth export controls — a significant concession given China controls approximately 60% of global rare earth processing capacity.

The wild card is the February 2026 Supreme Court ruling that constrained presidential tariff authority, shifting congressional leverage and boosting China's negotiating position. If the upcoming Trump-Xi summit produces a framework, $FXI could see a sharp re-rating. If it fails, rare earth controls coming back online would be the single biggest negative catalyst across all four ETFs.

Sector-by-sector read

$XLI (Industrials, $172.72): The standout. Domestic infrastructure spending via budget appropriations has more than offset tariff headwinds, making this the most defensible position in the basket right now.
